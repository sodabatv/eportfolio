My Reflection

 

Introduction

 

The article provides a summary of the journey that we embarked on throughout the duration of our module “Network and Information Security (NISM)” and for which the purpose was to give participants a strong foundation in computer network security concepts. The module comprised twelve weeklong units each with practical exercises to demonstrate the concepts as well technologies or tools that may be used to ensure cybersecurity.

 

Module Content

 

NISM started with the foundation; the four tenets of information security: Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability and Non-repudiation; a model used to evaluate the effectiveness of the security of a system. Other founding principles of information security; threats, vulnerabilities, and risk concepts, and the difference between each as the three are often used incorrectly. The concepts are the foundation of cybersecurity and a clear understanding of these was crucial. (Campbell, 2016). To validate the founding concepts, in unit two, there was case study where the STIRDE and DREAD modelling methods were used evaluate the security threats, vulnerabilities and risks in the electronic medical devices. Each group presented the findings during the second seminar. Modelling methods help moderate results, and this was one of the conclusions that could be derived from the presentations. (Hussain et al.)

 

Computer networking comes a long way.  Throughout the evolution, two models have featured prominently and have shaped today’s network: the OSI model and the TCP/IP framework. Hence, during NISM there was a discussion about the TCP/IP and the ISO/OSI protocol stacks and the difference between each. The two models both have their advantages and disadvantages. OSI is preferred over TCP/IP when the emphasis is on documentation, specification, and modularization while the TCP/IP model is    preferred for the implementation, reliability, and security of the network. (Kumar, 2014) (Russel, 2013)

 

Security starts with understanding the components that make up our network and, it is against this background that Unit 5 introduced various network discovery and analysis tools, their use cases and this included vulnerability scanning and intrusion prevention tools like firewalls as well as network topologies and their use. (Parziale et al) While Unit six discussed the evaluation of common network vulnerability scanning and penetration testing tools covering Metasploit, the Nessus Vulnerability Scanner, Nmap, Burp Suite, OWASP ZAP, SQLmap, Kali Linux, and Jawfish. Most of which are freely available and effective and therefore are recommended for use to help securing enterprise networks. An interesting take-home is that these are the same tools that bad actors would use to maliciously gain access to networks. (Geer, 2015)

 

Unit seven discussed the risk management in the context of information security and ways by which risks can be mitigated. In addition, we learnt about business continuity and its relationship with disaster recovery. Business continuity not only focuses on technology, people’s aspect, policies, and procedures are also vital. While the focus of Unit 8 was on the standards that define security compliance that organisations need to observe, including GDPR and PCI DISS being for data protection and privacy and for the protection of payment information respectively. There were also case studies to cement the understanding of the principles of the data protection legislation. In my view the GDPR guidelines would assist organisations improve information security and therefore, are recommended for adoption even for organisations outside their jurisdiction in the quest of ensuring cybersecurity. (ICO, 2020)

 

In Unit 9 covered logging and forensics and the important role each play in the event of a security breach. Logging tools discussed include Syslog-ng, Nagios, and Snort. Further, some open-source log analysis tools like Elasticsearch, ELK were introduced for consideration. Most of these tools are available as open source and commercial versions therefore enterprises may consider implementing a cost-effective solution based on them to enable breach detection and investigation. Lastly, we learnt about breach investigation checklist, and this was quite insightful as I we were introduced to a structured approach of investigating a breach. (SWIFT, 2010) (Eaton, 2003)

 

For unit 10, each participant was required to analyse a case study about a security breach that was experienced by some entities following the principles of breach investigation covered in the previous unit. For me this was a crash course into incident investigation, and I am better equipped to handle a preliminary investigation in future. Also worth considering is the forensic investigative skills for artifact and breach impact analysis. (Gwrispos et al, 2017)

 

Unit 11 zoned into security challenges of network today, secure protocols in use like IPSEC and virtual networking including Software Defined Networks (SDN). The second section introduced proposed future internet networking technologies like Mobilityfirst. As much as these developments are welcomed, the challenge for some is interoperability with existing internet technologies considering that some of the newbie’s intention is to completely replace them. The final unit mainly focused on the pros and cons of future technologies, and these were presented in the form of a debate where each team had to critique a certain proposition, and this allowed the participants to dig more into those technologies. (Ding, 2017)

 

Team Activities 

 

To fulfil the element of group work, three groups were established each comprising of at least four participants, following which we were then required to formulate the contract that will govern the groups. A brief overview of team activities is discussed below.

 

Individual Contributions

 

Two core assignments for the module; the design and the executive summary based on the allocated case study were undertaken in groups. Group three was allocated a customer management website www.customersrus.co.uk.

 

For the Design assignment, the requirement was to identify the appropriate body and regulations and from these, list of theoretical security vulnerabilities that could affect the website. Subsequently recommend potential mitigations to address vulnerabilities identified. I was assigned to document the possible tools that could be used to undertake the review of the security of the website and justification for each, and the group was happy with the contribution. 

 

For the executive summary assignment, the team assumed me to work on the discussion section of the assignment where we were required critique the results of our scan and compare them to the theoretical vulnerabilities identified during the design and to discuss why some of the theoretical vulnerabilities were not identified by the scanning tools. 

 

For both assignments, everyone in the group was involved in the layout, flow of the document and the correction of technical and grammatical errors. The assignment I did to the best of my ability.

 

 Experience Development Team

 

The two core activities are the core of what I do regularly at work and therefore, I had vulnerability management experience to share with the team. In that regard, the assignments validated what we do because we have been doing it without formal training. Another experience that I brought to the group was the design of controls to mitigate risks and my experience through implementation of ISMS. 

 

Impact Personal Development

 

Working in a diverse team like group three was an eye opener and is one of the positive outcomes out of the module. Group work sharpened my communication and persuasive skills as I had to convince the team occasionally to do something that initially to them seemed less beneficial but was crucial to the fulfilment of the assignment. The other aspect that improved is self-confidence and the ability to recognise strength and weaknesses of team players to get the best out of each one. All these are important soft skills that I will tap into in the future both in the workplace and personally considering that teamwork is core to virtually everything.

Collaborative Discussion Summaries

Collaborative discussion 1 Summary

 

The objective of the summary post is to provide an overview of the discussions that ensured regarding the medical mannequin case study the purpose of which was to highlight the cyber security concerns that could arise in a medical services organisation. (Glisson et al., 2015)

First, we were required to identify the vulnerabilities that were stated in the case study following which we were required to suggest possible safeguards that could be deployed to mitigate and therefore reduce the cyber security risks in a medical facility.

The initial post identified two areas of concern regarding cybersecurity in the case study. The first finding was that there were no structures in place to identify security threats within the facility. The other area of concern was that there was an absence of the security culture in the organisation.

To mitigate the risks, technological interventions could be implemented however as suggested in one of my peers’ responses the right way of going about it is to implement an Information Security Management System (ISMS). The system main purpose is to introduce a structed approach as well as risk-based safeguards to ensure cyber security. (Safonova, Kotelnikov, 2020)

ISMS implementation would also ensure that there is a security awareness programme in place to address the security culture that was found to be lacking in the organisation as well as help establish policies and procedures to address the structural concerns identified.

On lessons learned, cyber security is integral to the healthcare industry and therefore it is imperative for the industry to put it high on the agenda to ensure the integrity of the services they provide.

Collaborative Discussion 2 Summary

 

The post summarises the discussions that we have partook in following the network investigative scanning activities on the Group three website, www.customersrus.co.uk. Various tools were used.  The “traceroute” command was used to establish the number of hops required to reach the website from our locations. Other information such as the slowest links in the path and the average speed to reach the website were also obtained. The “whois” command was used for establishing the registered owner, authoritative Domain Name Servers as well as the location the website is operating from. For information about the mail server associated with the website, the “nslookup” command was used.

Also established during the exercise is that several tools should be used to validate the results and for more conclusive outcome, deep scanning tools like “nmap” should be considered. Further, the tools are also used by bad actors to perform reconnaissance attack our network resources therefore as security practitioners we should be on the lookout for such activities on our networks and defend against such. Intrusion Detection Systems can assist with the detection of such activity.

Collaborative Discussion 3 Summary

 

The post provides a summary of discussions in the collaborative learning discussion 3 where I was assigned to interrogate a GDPR principle of access to personal information by a third party. In my case the discussion was based on the “Personal data of a third party withheld from an access request made by the parent of a minor” case study of 2017. The general rule in this case is that personal data can only be shared with           third parties upon receipt of a consent from the owner of personal data. However, there are special circumstances where the consent may not be required due to among others safety and security concerns where there is a risk of harm to people.

The case study however was not considered to fall into the special circumstances referred and therefore the data controller or processor was not obliged to honour the request. There was a good suggestion about the consideration of the automation of the Data Subject Access Request to reduce the risk of access rights violations which I will duly recommend to data controllers and processors for implementation.

From the case study, the value of the GDPR in ensuring privacy is quite clear however there has to be special circumstances where parents to a minor should be exempted from consent in the interest of their minor children.
